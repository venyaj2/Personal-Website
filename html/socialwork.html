  • Emerging Identity of a Chinese Immigrant

    This paper delves into the life journey of Mr. C from adolescence to late adulthood. Through vivid storytelling and insightful analysis, it explores his upbringing in rural China, the challenges and triumphs of immigration to the United States, and the complexities of cultural adaptation and familial dynamics. Delving into themes of resilience, cultural fusion, and intergenerational connectivity, this paper offers a compelling narrative of hope, perseverance, and the enduring spirit of immigrants.

  • The Tropes of Misogyny in the Bollywood Film Industry

    This paper explores how misogynistic tropes have evolved in Indian cinema. The paper critically analyzes how female characters in films like Shree 420, Sholay, and Kabhi Khushi Kabhi Gham are shaped by societal and political changes, often reinforcing patriarchal norms. It offers insights into the industry's problematic stereotypes and highlights both positive and negative portrayals of women while examining their broader societal implications.

  • Quiet Room Exploration: The Level of Protection Granted to Illinois Students in Seclusion Setting Based on Development Patterns

    This study aims to explore the factors contributing to the acceptance of isolation room usage, particularly focusing on geographic and demographic factors. It reviews existing literature, including investigative reports like ProPublica's collaboration with the Chicago Tribune, which revealed widespread use of quiet rooms in Illinois schools, sometimes against the law.
